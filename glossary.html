  • Weather
  • the state of the atmosphere at a place and time as regards heat, dryness, sunshine, wind, rain, etc.
  • Time
    • Epoch Time
    • This is the time in seconds Since January 1, 1970 Used for absolute computational time, giving all computers a common reference point for time
    • Local Time
  • Condition Code
  • The way that the system tracks the weather at a location, using a number to denote certain conditions.
  • Is Day?
  • This denotes whether it is Day. 0 means it is night, 1 means that it is day.
  • Is Windy?
  • This denotes whether the weather can be flagged as windy or not. Based on local wind speed
  • Is Cloudy?
  • Denotes whether the weather is considered cloudy for this location
  • Pressure
  • Atmospheric pressure, which can be influence by temperature and humidity
    • Millibars
    • Means of Measuring the pressure, in mercury. 1 bar is equal to 1/100,000 of a Pascal, or 1 Newton / Square Meter
    • Inches
    • Inches that Mercury rises under pressure. Because mercury is very dense, it is used to measure pressure.
  • Humidity
  • A measure of the water vapor in the air. 0% means completely Dry, 100% means rain.
  • Ultraviolet Index
  • A measure of the intensity of the UV rays form the sun, on a scale of 1 (low risk) to 10 (Very high risk). The higher the number, the more dangerous.
